# Brand Voice Guardrail

Use this as a required pre-flight for any customer-facing words produced by Fig & Bloom, Bower, or agents working on the reorg growth plan.

## Trigger

Use when work includes:

- website, email, SMS, ad, social, PR, partnership, or creator copy;
- agent SOUL or AGENTS.md instructions that define brand behaviour;
- product, bundle, program, or occasion naming;
- support, sympathy, bereavement, corporate, or funeral-director messaging.

## Non-Negotiables

- Australian English only: `colour`, `organise`, `favour`, `mum`, `towards`.
- No exclamation marks in customer-facing copy unless a human explicitly approves the exact line.
- Avoid banned words in customer copy: `luxury`, `elevate`, `curate`, `stunning`, `gorgeous`, `blooms`.
- Do not describe Fig & Bloom as letterbox, cheap, discount-led, or volume-led.
- Prefer design-led, care-led, and occasion-specific language over generic florist claims.
- Keep copy quiet, exact, and emotionally literate. Remove hype before polishing.
- Do not overpromise delivery, freshness, availability, recipient reaction, or partnership status.

## Review Workflow

1. Identify brand, channel, audience, occasion, and sensitivity level.
2. Scan for banned words, American spelling, exclamation marks, hype, and generic CTAs.
3. Check the strategic job:
   - Fig & Bloom: design-led gifting that makes the sender feel certain the gesture landed.
   - Bower: value-conscious flowers without looking cheap, copied, or discount-store.
4. Rewrite any line that sounds salesy, performatively cheerful, or abstractly premium.
5. For sensitive occasions, run the stricter sympathy/bereavement standard:
   - plain language;
   - no cheerleading;
   - no bright-side framing;
   - no assumed relationship, faith, or grief stage.

## Escalation

Escalate to the CEO only when a requested voice rule would change brand positioning, allow banned words as a campaign norm, or approve customer-facing grief/sympathy copy that fails the sensitivity standard.
