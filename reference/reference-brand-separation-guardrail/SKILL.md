# Brand Separation Guardrail

Bower can compete on value without diluting Fig & Bloom. This skill keeps the two brands distinct across copy, creative, offers, reporting, and operational decisions.

## Trigger

Use when work involves:

- Bower launch, Bower offers, Bower bundles, or Bower paid media;
- Fig & Bloom and Bower appearing in the same plan, report, asset library, campaign calendar, or customer journey;
- value-bundle decisions, price matching, Daily Blooms comparisons, or partner bundles;
- shared creative assets, shared customer data, or cross-brand retargeting.

## Separation Rules

| Area | Fig & Bloom | Bower |
|---|---|---|
| Core job | Design-led gifting certainty | Value-conscious flowers with taste |
| Price posture | Worth paying for | Fair price, clean offer |
| Creative | Editorial, occasion-led, crafted | Tactile, direct, simple, value clear |
| Copy | Warm, restrained, emotional precision | Plain, confident, useful |
| Offers | Credit, added care, program value | Bundles, transparent value, simple thresholds |

Do not let Bower borrow Fig & Bloom's premium proof points unless explicitly true for Bower. Do not let Fig & Bloom adopt discount-led language because Bower exists.

## Review Workflow

1. Identify which brand owns the customer promise.
2. Check whether creative assets, copy, products, prices, audiences, and reporting labels are brand-specific.
3. Check for accidental brand leakage:
   - Fig & Bloom using Bower value language;
   - Bower using Fig & Bloom premium service claims;
   - shared assets with the wrong logo, tone, colour, offer, or product metadata.
4. For bundles, record product source, fulfilment owner, margin owner, and customer-service owner.
5. For reporting, keep CAC, payback, LTV, and margin separated unless a blended number is explicitly requested and labelled.

## Escalation

Escalate to the CEO before changing the strategic relationship between Fig & Bloom and Bower, combining brand P&Ls, or making cross-brand customer promises that affect board workflow.
