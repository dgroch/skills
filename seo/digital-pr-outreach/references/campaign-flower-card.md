# Campaign Reference — "What Australians Really Write on a Flower Card"

Worked example of a digital PR campaign. Use as a blueprint for the next data-journalism story built on Fig & Bloom first-party data.

## The Story
A Sydney florist analysed 12 months of real flower card messages (~40k orders) and discovered what Australians are really feeling — but not saying out loud. Original first-party data, not a repackaged survey.

## Source
https://figandbloom.com/blogs/news/what-australians-write-on-a-flower-card

## Core Data Points
- **93%** of orders included a written message (only 1 in 14 left it blank)
- **Average length:** ~29 words; longest exceeded 400 words
- **Most common phrase:** "Thinking of you" (1 in 7 cards) — beats "Happy birthday" and "Love you"
- **Love vs Sorry:** "Love you" written ~2x as often as "Sorry"
- **1 in 3** messages end with kisses ("xx" favourite, some 7+)
- **Emojis:** only ~6% of messages; almost always a heart — "stubbornly handwritten"
- **Sympathy + Support = 23%** combined

## Memorable Real Messages (anonymised)
- *"I miss you."* (the entire message)
- *"Thinking of you today, Mum, the day he touched earth and heaven. He is never forgotten."*
- *"You did it. You crushed the hardest day you'll ever have. I love you endlessly, and I'm so proud of you."*
- *"Happy birthday to our most favourite aunty ever. We know you don't love getting flowers, they aren't recycled and will die, but we love you, it's your birthday."*

## Prioritisation Lesson
Craig Butt is the most precise beat match but his data-desk pieces don't reach flower-buyers. **Lead with consumer-facing outlets** (Jana Hocking/news.com.au, Mamamia, ABC Everyday, Time Out/Broadsheet gift guides). Demote data desks and .edu targets to a secondary wave for authority and SEO, not as the spearhead.
