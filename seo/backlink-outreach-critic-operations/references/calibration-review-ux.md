# Daniel Calibration Review UX

Use this format whenever asking Daniel to label backlink outreach, routing, or copy.

## Required self-contained brief

Do not assume Daniel has opened Trello, Notion, local artifacts, or prior agent output. Every review prompt must state:

1. Publisher and relevant source article/page, with direct links.
2. The exact opportunity in one sentence.
3. Desired business outcome (editorial mention, backlink, quote, contribution, collaboration, etc.).
4. Why Fig & Bloom is a credible fit.
5. Evidence, unresolved facts, and material risks.
6. The exact proposed next action.
7. Full proposed outreach copy when copy is being judged.
8. Explicit confirmation that approval will or will not trigger a send.

## Split decisions by dimension

Never combine routing and copy approval into one verdict. Ask in this order:

1. **Opportunity/routing:** pursue, research further, hold, or escalate?
2. **Copy:** approve unchanged, revise, reject, or defer until recipient research?

A publisher being prominent is not sufficient for escalation. Escalate only for a concrete legal, financial, reputational, authority, or commitment boundary. If the opportunity is sound but the recipient is unresolved, route to autonomous contact research rather than `Needs Daniel`.

## Interaction format

- Use clickable choices via the clarification tool.
- Present one decision at a time.
- Keep button labels explicit about the effect.
- If the question expires, resume from the last persisted label without restarting the batch.
- Persist Daniel's answer immediately with stable event ID, source run, opportunity key, decision dimension, system verdict, Daniel verdict, exact feedback, and whether any external action occurred.

## Example sequence

**Routing:** `Should the AI pursue this opportunity after finding a current editorial contact?`

**Copy:** `Is this exact message sendable once that contact is verified?`

Do not ask vague questions such as “What is your calibration verdict?” without first defining exactly what is being judged.
