# Fig & Bloom Creator Brand And Aesthetic Rubric

Use this shared rubric across Hashgifted shortlist, selection, monitoring, and closeout.

## Core Fit

Fig & Bloom creator partnerships should feel premium, considered, warm, and emotionally believable. Strong fits usually sit near:

- lifestyle
- home
- family
- beauty
- fashion
- interiors
- food
- events
- gifting
- emotionally warm personal moments

## Positive Signals

- Strong visual consistency.
- Creator voice feels personal and grounded.
- Audience likely includes people buying flowers for the campaign occasion.
- Real comments and healthy engagement relative to follower count.
- Ability to show an emotional or visual story without heavy direction.
- Natural warmth rather than polished ad-speak.

## On-Brand But Easy To Misread

Do not reject solely for:

- glamour
- beauty-focus
- body-confidence
- sensual-but-tasteful content

These can be on-brand when the creator's world still feels premium, personal, and emotionally compatible with floral gifting.

## Weak Or Off-Brand Signals

Decline or manual-review creators dominated by:

- gambling-heavy content
- crypto or scam-adjacent promotion
- controversial politics or conspiracy content
- overtly laddish humour
- gaming or streaming with no lifestyle/gifting angle
- chaotic visual identity with no strong relevant posts

## Engagement Judgement

Prefer micro-creators with real engagement over larger creators with weak comments. Do not chase follower count when engagement and fit are poor.

## Campaign Objective Lens

For `audience awareness`, prioritise audience relevance, local or demographic alignment, trust, and engagement.

For `content library`, prioritise visual quality, consistency, product/home/lifestyle setting, and ability to create usable photo/video assets.

For `both`, require at least medium strength on audience relevance and content quality.
