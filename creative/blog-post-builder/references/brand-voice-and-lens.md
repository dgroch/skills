# Brand voice + drafting lens — self-contained copy

Condensed from the Fig & Bloom Parent Editorial Framework and the Blog Post
Thought-Leader Lens Workflow (Notion), so the skill works even when Notion is
unreachable. Notion is authoritative if the two ever diverge.

## Brand voice (the five "X, not Y")

- **Premium, not pretentious** — quiet confidence; specificity over superlatives
  ("hand-tied this morning, delivered by three this afternoon", not "the best
  flowers in Melbourne").
- **Thoughtful, not precious** — care shown through useful attentiveness, not
  whimsy.
- **Australian, authentically** — Australian English and rhythm; direct but
  warm; trust understatement. Say "have a look" not "take a peek"; "we'll sort
  it" not "we've got you covered". Avoid Americanisms ("super cute", "obsessed")
  and UK affectations ("rather lovely").
- **Clear, not salesy** — lead with clarity; never manufacture urgency or lead
  with discounts.
- **Warm, not gushing** — warmth from specificity and attentiveness, not volume
  or exclamation marks.

## Core creative principles

1. **Emotional Certainty** — lead with the feeling the recipient will have;
   answer the sender's unspoken *"Will this feel right?"*.
2. **Craft Proof** — visible evidence of how things are made (hand-tied,
   season-led, the 6am market run) — reassurance, never a lecture.
3. **Delivery Confidence** — show arrivals (the knock, the unboxing, placement
   on a table); logistics becoming emotion.

## Messaging hierarchy (every piece, in order)

1. **Emotional arc (lead)** — who's sending, who's receiving, the feeling
   between them. The entry point.
2. **Craft + delivery proof (support)** — the reassurance Fig & Bloom will
   execute the intention.
3. **Product + practical details (close)** — what it is, what it costs, how to
   order. Always present, never leading.

> First sentence should make a reader *feel* something; the whole thing should
> tell them exactly what to do next.

## Tone guardrails — avoid → use instead

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Spoil them / yourself" | "Send something that feels right" |
| "Stunning / gorgeous / breathtaking" | specific descriptors: "deep burgundy ranunculus", "soft, loose garden roses" |
| "Order now! / Don't miss out!" | "We deliver same-day across Melbourne if you order by noon." |
| "Cheap / affordable / budget-friendly" | "From $89" — let price speak |
| "One-stop shop / perfect for every occasion" | name the specific occasion and why this arrangement suits it |
| "Florist-grade / professional quality" | the actual process: "selected at market this morning" |
| "Say it with flowers" | the actual message: "Tell them you're thinking of them, even from a distance." |

Also: **don't lead with discounts** (mention promos plainly, no fanfare),
**kill florist clichés** ("beautiful blooms", "freshly picked", "handcrafted
with love" — every adjective must do specific work), and **don't over-sell**
(one CTA, not five; trust the reader).

## The 7-step drafting workflow (machinery, never named in copy)

Default lens stack: **Holmes** (be useful for the 97% not buying today) ·
**Schwartz** (match headline/opening to awareness) · **Godin** (Moment-Maker
identity) · **Ziglar** (warmth/sincerity) · **Ogilvy** (specifics + brand
quality control). Specialist overlays only where the post calls for it: Tony
Robbins (get-well/grief/celebration), Cialdini (proof/trust/review-led),
Hormozi (bundles/subscriptions/offers), Halbert (urgent occasion pages, not the
dominant blog voice), Joe Girard (reminder/relationship flows).

1. **Identify the moment** — occasion/context, sender role, recipient state,
   what the sender fears getting wrong, what a good outcome feels like.
2. **Pick awareness (Schwartz)** — product-aware / problem-aware / feeling-aware
   / lifestyle-unaware. Sets headline + opening.
3. **Make it useful (Holmes)** — reduce one real anxiety or teach one practical
   decision. Must answer "what should I do now?".
4. **Moment-Maker identity (Godin)** — "people like us notice, remember, thank,
   comfort, celebrate with intention".
5. **Humanise (Ziglar)** — remove cleverness that gets in the way; sound like a
   thoughtful friend.
6. **Specificity pass (Ogilvy)** — named bouquets, palette/composition, delivery
   reality, vase/card/add-on pathway, florist craft. No vague filler.
7. **CTA by intent** — product-aware → shop / choose delivery date;
   problem-aware → recommendation path / occasion guide / card wording;
   feeling-aware → "send the gesture" / "mark the moment"; lifestyle-unaware →
   save the guide / join list / explore ritual or hosting category.

## Cross-channel note (blog register)

Blog voice is **authoritative, generous, educational** — give value before
asking for anything. Teach (how to write a card message, how to choose for an
occasion), share (process, seasonal availability), position Fig & Bloom as the
thoughtful expert. Long-form is fine when genuinely useful — something a reader
would bookmark or share.

## Pre-publish consistency checklist

- [ ] Same voice as every other channel?
- [ ] Emotional arc leads, craft + product support?
- [ ] A stranger understands what Fig & Bloom stands for within one sentence?
- [ ] At least one specific detail (stem name, delivery time, real occasion)?
- [ ] No discount language, florist clichés, or over-selling?
- [ ] Australian English throughout?
- [ ] Helps before it sells, with a clear next action?
