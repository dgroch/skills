# Bower — Scoring Rubric

The base critic rubric (lighting, composition, colour, styling,
technical, brand_alignment) is defined in `brand.json` →
`critic.dimensions` and applies to **every** Bower image. This file
captures additional scoring criteria for shot types that need lane-
specific judgement on top of the base rubric.

---

## Hero Editorial

Applies to website-only hero images (see `art-direction.md` →
"Hero Editorial" and `grid-spec.md` → "Website-only lanes").

Score these alongside the base rubric. A hero-editorial shot can hit
9s on lighting and colour and still fail this lane if it reads as
product photography.

| Dimension                  | What scores high                                                                                                                                              | What scores low                                                                                          |
|----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Atmosphere and mood        | Mood reads before the arrangement does. Light, air, and warmth carry the frame. The viewer feels the room before they catalogue the flowers.                 | Image is technically clean but emotionally flat. Reads as a catalogue shot with a window behind it.      |
| Narrative implication      | The scene suggests a moment — someone just left, a meal in progress, morning light through a kitchen. There's an implied story without anyone in frame.        | Scene is static and unpopulated by implication. No sense that anything happened here or is about to.     |
| Editorial cropping         | The crop would be wrong for a PDP — tight, off-centre, partial. Vase clipped, bloom sliced by the edge, arrangement pushed to the lower third. **Good.**       | Centred, generous-around-the-vase composition that could double as a product page hero.                  |
| Arrangement integration    | Reads as part of the scene — incidental, lived-in, as if the photographer was shooting the room and the flowers happened to be there.                          | Reads as staged into the scene — props arranged around an obvious centrepiece, lighting favouring blooms.|
| No models / no faces       | Human traces only: a hand leaving frame, a half-poured glass, a folded napkin, a chair pushed back. (Restates the existing brand-wide rule for this lane.)     | Any face, any identifiable model, any front-and-centre human subject.                                    |

**Verdict guidance:**
- All five dimensions ≥ 7 and base rubric pass → PASS.
- Any dimension ≤ 5 → ITERATE with specific direction toward the
  failing axis (e.g. "recompose with vase clipped at the right edge,
  push arrangement to lower-left third").
- A face, model, or identifiable human in frame → automatic FAIL on
  the no-models dimension regardless of other scores.
