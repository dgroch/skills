## Primary Palette (60/30/10 rule)

| Name       | Hex      | Ratio | Description                            | Usage                                           |
|------------|----------|-------|----------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Linen      | #EDE3D5  | 60%   | Sun-warmed linen with honey undertone  | Backgrounds, negative space, packaging exterior |
| Terracotta | #BF6B4A  | 30%   | Fired clay with copper warmth          | Logo, CTA buttons, accent lines, price          |
| Espresso   | #2B2220  | 10%   | Roasted espresso — warm dark, not black| Body text, headings, UI labels, icon strokes    |

## Secondary Palette

| Name       | Hex      | Description                              | Usage                                         |
|------------|----------|------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Sandstone  | #D4B896  | Golden sandstone — warm mid-tone         | Secondary backgrounds, card surfaces, hover   |
| Eucalyptus | #7E9187  | Dried eucalyptus leaf — cool counterpoint| Supporting accents, tags, badges, tissue paper |
| Amber      | #D96B3A  | Bright amber with burnt orange energy    | Sale badges, urgency CTAs, limited-edition     |

## Forbidden Pairings

- **Terracotta + Amber** — Too close in hue, vibrates
- **Sandstone + Linen** — Insufficient contrast, elements disappear
- **Terracotta + Eucalyptus** — Competing mid-tones with no anchor

## Anti-patterns

- Baby pink as primary — category default, not this brand
- All-pastel palette — reads as gift shop, not lifestyle
- Black-and-gold luxury signalling — wrong price bracket
- Two saturated colours competing — Amber and Terracotta never share a layout

## Key Rule for Photography Prompts

When describing colour in prompts, use the palette language: "warm linen-cream
tones" not "white"; "terracotta" not "orange"; "sage-eucalyptus greens" not
"green"; "espresso" not "black". This keeps AI generations tonally consistent.
