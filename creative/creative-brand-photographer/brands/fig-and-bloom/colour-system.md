# Fig & Bloom — Colour System

---

## Brand Palette (Monochrome + Clay)

The brand palette is deliberately restrained — monochrome with a single
warm neutral. The FLOWERS provide all colour. This is a strategic choice:
the brand frame is minimal and sophisticated, letting the product be the
visual event.

| Name       | Hex      | Usage                                              |
|------------|----------|----------------------------------------------------|
| Noir       | #000000  | Logo, primary text, ribbon, graphic tiles           |
| Silk White | #FFFFFF  | Logo reverse, tissue wrap, clean backgrounds        |
| Clay       | #D8CCBE  | Studio backdrop, secondary backgrounds, card stock  |
| Clay 50%   | #EBE5DF  | Light variant — email canvas, subtle backgrounds    |

## Photography Colour — The Flowers ARE the Palette

Unlike brands with a fixed accent colour, Fig & Bloom's visual colour
rotates seasonally through the flower palette. The brand constraint is:
flower colours must always be vivid and true-to-life against the neutral
brand frame.

### Core Flower Colours (recurring across seasons)

| Colour             | Appears as                    | Prompt language                     |
|--------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Orchid pink        | Phalaenopsis orchids          | "vivid orchid pink"                 |
| Fuchsia            | Orchids, gypsophila, stock    | "saturated fuchsia"                 |
| Amber gold         | Garden roses                  | "warm amber roses"                  |
| Coral              | Anthurium, ranunculus         | "coral anthurium"                   |
| Lilac / purple     | Stock, lisianthus             | "purple stock, soft lilac"          |
| Blush              | Garden roses, peonies         | "blush rose, soft peach"            |
| Cream white        | Chrysanthemums, roses, baby's breath | "cream chrysanthemum"        |
| Sage green         | Eucalyptus, foliage           | "sage eucalyptus, natural foliage"  |

### Seasonal Emphasis

- **Spring/Summer:** Orchid pink, amber, fuchsia, coral — maximum vibrancy
- **Autumn/Winter:** Blush, cream, burgundy, dried textures — warmer, richer
- **Campaign moments:** Can introduce one bold seasonal colour (deep red for Valentine's, sunflower gold for Mother's Day)

## Forbidden Combinations

- **Muted/desaturated flowers on clay backdrop** — reads as washed out; flowers must pop
- **Black backdrop + dark flowers** — loses the brand's warmth and optimism
- **Competing saturated colours in model's outfit AND bouquet** — the model is neutral, the flowers bring the colour
- **Neon or artificially dyed flowers** — must read as natural, even when vivid

## Anti-Patterns

- All-pastel, desaturated flower palette — this isn't Bower; Fig & Bloom flowers are vivid
- Over-warm colour grade that shifts whites to yellow — tissue should read clean white
- Cool/blue colour temperature — kills the clay backdrop warmth
- High-contrast B&W treatment on photography — the monochrome palette is for graphics, not photos

## Key Rule for Photography Prompts

When describing colour in prompts, use vivid, specific flower names: "fuchsia
phalaenopsis orchids" not "pink flowers"; "amber garden roses" not "yellow
roses"; "coral anthurium" not "red flower." The backdrop is always described as
"warm clay-toned studio backdrop" — never "beige" or "brown." Model clothing
uses "noir" and "silk white" — not "black" and "white."
