# Fig & Bloom — Instagram Grid Specification

---

## 9-Post Composition Pattern

The grid alternates between three content lanes: editorial photography,
graphic tiles, and product/botanical shots. The overall impression when
viewing the grid should be: fashion magazine meets premium florist.

```
┌──────────────────┬──────────────────┬──────────────────┐
│  Fashion hero    │  Graphic quote   │  Product detail  │
│  (editorial)     │  (B&W tile)      │  (bouquet)       │
├──────────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Fashion full-body│  Botanical close │  Lifestyle scene │
│  (editorial)     │  (product)       │  (tablescape)    │
├──────────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│  Graphic review  │  Fashion profile │  Product wrapped │
│  (B&W tile)      │  (editorial)     │  (bouquet)       │
└──────────────────┴──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

### Content Lanes (3 types)

| Lane      | Description                                                | Ratio  |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Editorial | Fashion-styled model holding bouquet, clay backdrop         | 3:4    |
| Graphic   | B&W quote tiles, review cards, illustration tiles           | 1:1    |
| Product   | Bouquet close-ups, botanical details, wrapped product shots | 1:1/3:4|

### Grid Rules

**Colour rhythm:** Editorial shots bring vivid flower colour. Graphic
tiles are monochrome (Noir + Silk White). Product shots sit between —
flowers are vivid but framing is tighter and calmer. This creates a
rhythm of colour intensity: vivid → monochrome → medium → vivid.

**Never two graphics adjacent:** Graphic tiles should be separated by
at least one photography post. Two B&W tiles next to each other kills
the visual energy.

**Editorial is the anchor:** At least 3 of every 9 posts should be
fashion editorial (model + bouquet). These are the brand's hero content
and the most shareable.

**Diversity across editorial:** No two adjacent editorial posts should
feature the same model or the same bouquet composition. Vary model,
outfit, bouquet colour story, and framing.

## Shot Types by Lane

### Editorial Lane (3 per 9-post cycle)

| Slot              | Shot type        | Ratio | Notes                                   |
|-------------------|------------------|-------|-----------------------------------------|
| Fashion hero      | Three-quarter    | 3:4   | Primary hero — model + bouquet, clay bg |
| Fashion full-body | Full-length      | 3:4   | Full outfit visible, bouquet at hip     |
| Fashion profile   | Side/profile     | 3:4   | Model turned, bouquet prominent         |

### Product Lane (3 per 9-post cycle)

| Slot              | Shot type        | Ratio | Notes                                   |
|-------------------|------------------|-------|-----------------------------------------|
| Product detail    | Bouquet close-up | 1:1   | Tight crop on flowers, tissue visible   |
| Botanical close   | Macro/detail     | 1:1   | Single flower variety, shallow DOF      |
| Product wrapped   | Full bouquet     | 3:4   | Bouquet on surface, wrapping as hero    |

### Graphic Lane (3 per 9-post cycle)

| Slot              | Type             | Ratio | Notes                                   |
|-------------------|------------------|-------|-----------------------------------------|
| Graphic quote     | Quote tile       | 1:1   | Lust Display headline, Noir bg or white |
| Graphic review    | Customer review  | 1:1   | Review text, monochrome + illustration  |
| (flex)            | Illustration/CTA | 1:1   | Line art, seasonal message, hiring      |

*Note: Graphic tiles are NOT generated by the photographer skill. They
are created via the creative-add-creative-builder skill. The grid spec
includes them for rhythm planning.*

## Stories vs Feed

**Feed:** Curated, editorial, high-production. Every post is considered.
The feed should look like a fashion magazine's Instagram account.

**Stories:** Can be warmer and more behind-the-scenes. Studio prep,
bouquet assembly process, wrapping close-ups, model arrival. Still
visually polished — no shaky phone footage or poor lighting — but
the energy is more intimate and process-oriented.

---

## Channel-Specific Guidance

**Instagram Feed:** 3:4 or 1:1 only. Never 16:9 in feed.
**Instagram Stories:** 9:16. Can use editorial shots with text overlay.
**Website Hero:** 16:9 wide crop from editorial shoots. Model at
one-third, bouquet at two-thirds, negative space for headline overlay.
**Email Header:** 3:1 or 2:1 banner crop. Bouquet-focused, warm backdrop.
**Ads (Meta):** 4:5 vertical from editorial shots. High-colour bouquet
as stopping power.
