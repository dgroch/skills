# Fig & Bloom — Brand Personas

Five core personas. Use these when selecting campaign targets, writing tone notes, or inferring product relevance.

---

## 1. The Everyday Aesthete

**Trigger contexts:** Subscription campaigns, seasonal/native ranges, editorial content, evergreen lifestyle sends.

**Profile:** Inner-city Melbourne, 28–45. Her home is intentional — good linen, considered objects, natural light. Flowers aren't decoration; they're part of how she lives. She's not buying blooms, she's maintaining a standard.

**Motivation:** Identity and environment, not occasion.

**Product fit:** Subscription-native. Seasonal and native-led ranges. Values variety and surprise within a trusted aesthetic. Willing to pay premium for something unavailable at a supermarket.

**Channel:** Instagram, editorial content, home/lifestyle adjacent.

**Messaging angle:** Living well, not gifting. She's the brand ambassador persona — she shares, she recommends.

**One-line summary:** She buys flowers the way she buys good olive oil — because her life requires it.

---

## 2. The Considered Giver

**Trigger contexts:** Premium gifting campaigns, "for someone who has everything" sends, editorial gifting guides, Moonpig upper-end.

**Profile:** Sends flowers specifically because they've chosen Fig & Bloom — the brand is part of the gift. They're expressing their own taste through the act of giving. The recipient might not know the difference, but they do.

**Motivation:** The gift reflects on them. Quality and specificity, not just a nice gesture.

**Product fit:** Premium single arrangements, thoughtful add-ons (notes, pairings). Doesn't need occasion prompting. Would use saved address book / easy re-order.

**Channel:** Word of mouth, gifting guides, high-consideration content.

**Messaging angle:** "For someone who has everything." Not price-sensitive but hates feeling overcharged.

**One-line summary:** The brand is the signal — they chose us because it says something about them.

---

## 3. The Milestone Sender

**Trigger contexts:** Mother's Day, Valentine's Day, birthday, anniversary, new baby, sympathy, congratulations, any occasion-triggered campaign. Moonpig partnership sweet spot.

**Profile:** Reaches for flowers at the big moments. Chooses Fig & Bloom because the occasion warrants something elevated, not because they're deeply brand-loyal.

**Motivation:** The right gesture, reliably delivered. They want to feel like they got it right.

**Product fit:** Clear occasion framing, reliable fulfilment, easy ordering. Sympathy arrangements deserve a separate quiet category. Highest-volume gifting persona.

**Channel:** SEO, occasion-triggered paid search, retargeting.

**Messaging angle:** Confidence and ease — "you can trust this." Retargeting works well (they return for the next milestone).

**One-line summary:** They're not a regular customer — they're a recurring one. Each milestone brings them back.

---

## 4. The Entertainer

**Trigger contexts:** Spring/summer seasonal sends, "for the table" content, event-adjacent campaigns, dinner party / hosting occasions.

**Profile:** Hosts well and knows it. Dinner parties, long lunches, celebrations at home. Flowers are part of the whole environment they're composing for their guests.

**Motivation:** Flowers as part of the experience, not an afterthought.

**Product fit:** Volume options, lead-time flexibility, table arrangement framing. Seasonal and event-adjacent bundles. Could convert to subscription after a positive first experience.

**Channel:** Pinterest, recipe/lifestyle content, Melbourne food/hospitality account crossover.

**Messaging angle:** "For the table." Environment curation, not floral decoration.

**One-line summary:** She's composing an experience for her guests — flowers are a prop in a bigger scene she's directing.

---

## 5. The Trade Buyer

**Trigger contexts:** B2B campaigns, account onboarding, subscription renewal, commercial partnership sends.

**Profile:** Office managers, EAs, venue and hospitality buyers. Buying at volume, probably on a schedule. Flowers are part of the environment they manage, not a personal expression.

**Motivation:** Consistent quality, no surprises, easy re-ordering.

**Product fit:** Subscription-native but commercially structured (invoicing, account management). Less reactive to aesthetic trends; more sensitive to reliability and service.

**Channel:** Essentially sales-led. LinkedIn, direct outreach, hospitality network referrals. Melbourne venue testimonials carry real weight.

**Messaging angle:** Reliability and service. Not content-led — relationship-led.

**One-line summary:** They don't care about the story — they care that it arrives right, every time.
