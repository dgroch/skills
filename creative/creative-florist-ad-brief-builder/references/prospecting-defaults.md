# Florist Prospecting Defaults

Apply these defaults unless the task explicitly overrides them.

## Campaign intent

- Objective: prospecting / first-order acquisition
- Audience state: cold or lightly aware
- Product framing: bouquet or gifting range, not brand manifesto
- Conversion goal: click to browse, shop, or order

## Default promise hierarchy

Use one primary promise per brief:
1. Easy gifting for a specific recipient or moment
2. Same-day or local delivery confidence
3. Price discoverability or range clarity
4. Premium feel without overcomplication

## Strong florist prospecting mechanics

- Open with a recipient or occasion cue
- Make the shopping action obvious in the first beat
- Show range, price, or delivery benefit early
- Use native vertical sequences over polished brand-film pacing
- Keep one claim per concept

## Preferred recipient frames

- `For your mum`
- `For your partner`
- `For a last-minute save`
- `For birthdays this week`
- `For when words are not enough`

Use these as starting points, then tighten them to the actual brief.

## Proof point priorities

Prefer concrete proof points in this order:
1. same-day or next-day delivery
2. order cutoff or delivery area
3. starting price or price tier
4. best-seller or range clarity
5. add-on or card-message personalization

## Visual defaults

- Format: 9:16 vertical first
- First frames: bouquet, handoff, unwrapping, doorstep, price card, or swipeable range
- Capture style: native phone footage, creator-style framing, simple overlays
- Landing continuity: ad promise must match the page users land on

## Brand conversion rule

Pattern borrowing is allowed. The brief must still inject Fig & Bloom cues:
- premium but not loud
- warm, understated, editorial language
- explicit note of brand seeds or visual world references from Google Drive
- no leftover competitor names, offers, or category language
