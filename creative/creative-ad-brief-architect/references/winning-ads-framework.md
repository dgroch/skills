# Winning Ads Framework — Pattern Interrupt → Proof

A five-step structure for prospecting video, adapted to Fig & Bloom's register.
We keep the skeleton; we re-skin the tone. Source: "The Psychology of Winning
Ads" (The Moonlighters), a direct-response framework for Meta performance video.
We adopt the bones and translate the texture into considered, sender-first
warmth. One occasion, one intent per piece.

Mirrors the canonical Notion playbook. If they diverge, Notion wins — re-pull it.

## The five steps

1. **Pattern interrupt** — stop the right person in the first seconds, not with a
   flash but by calling out the avatar and opening a curiosity gap. The named
   bouquet is visible by ~second 2 so feeling and product land together.
   *Watch-out: no shock-for-shock's-sake, no urgency. Recognition, not noise.*

2. **Self-recognition** — name one specific avatar and drill it so the viewer
   feels the ad is for them. Avatar = a kind of person and mindset, not a
   demographic. *Watch-out: don't hedge across two avatars. One reader, one
   moment.* (Steps 1 and 2 fire together — the callout is the recognition.)

3. **Problem articulation** — name the avatar's real doubt. The category enemy is
   **careless gifting**: a gift that turns up small, tired, nothing like the
   photo, or feels too small for the moment. Frame as the sender's doubt, never a
   rival, never clinically. *Watch-out: state the fear plainly, then move; don't
   itemise like a DR list.*

4. **Unique mechanism** — the real, named Fig & Bloom design: designed, not
   assembled — hand-tied, arriving looking exactly like the photo, still
   beautiful in seven days. The named design **is** the mechanism. *Watch-out:
   real designs only. Any falling-short gift shown in a hook is deliberately not
   a Fig & Bloom design.*

5. **Proof** — split into social proof and risk reversal. The **message back**
   (the call, the photo on the table) is our social proof of the moment after.
   Risk reversal is the offer stack (Free Size Upgrade behind the 7-Day Freshness
   Promise) — value, never discount. *Watch-out: no fabricated testimonials or
   unverified review counts. For tender occasions, let presence be the proof and
   hold the guarantee as a quiet closing reassurance, not a super.*

## The hook principle (most often got wrong)

The pattern interrupt works by speaking directly to one avatar and voicing
something they **already feel or think** — so a true audience match recognises
themselves and stops. Present tense, second person, aimed at them; never the
talent narrating themselves.

It can take many shapes — pick whatever lands the recognition:

- Name a feeling they're holding — "You're still waiting for someone to send them."
- Voice a private thought — "A text feels too small for this."
- State a tension — "You want to be in the room. You're three states away."
  (you want X / you've got Y — **one shape, not the rule**.)
- Reflect a belief or identity — "You're the one who always remembers."

✅ Right (voicing their state): "You're still waiting for someone to send them."
❌ Wrong (talent narrating self): "I've never been the flowers guy."

The test isn't the template — it's resonance: **would the right person feel
that's me?**

## Fig & Bloom translation — keep / drop

| Drop (the DR texture) | Keep (the structure) |
|---|---|
| Shock hooks, flashes, backflips | Avatar callout in the first seconds |
| Generic "everyone" targeting | One avatar, drilled hard |
| Blunt problem laundry-lists | Naming the real problem |
| Split-screen demo clichés | A clear unique mechanism |
| Urgency, countdowns, discounts | Proof + risk reversal |
| Hype, performative reactions | One clear CTA (Shop Now) |

> The Whoosh test: if a hook or line would also work on a Whoosh ad, it isn't
> ours yet.

## Pre-ship checklist

1. Hook is present-tense, second person, spoken to one named avatar — voicing
   what they already feel or think (any shape; not a fixed template).
2. Named Fig & Bloom design is the hero and the fix — no generic flowers, and the
   hero beat is tagged REAL HERO SHOT REQUIRED.
3. Problem is the sender's doubt — no rival named, no clinical list.
4. Mechanism shows why it's built different (designed-not-assembled, arrives like
   the photo, 7-day).
5. Proof = message back + value-stacked offer; no fabricated proof.
6. Feeling leads, logistics support; value not discount; AU English; calm
   punctuation.
7. Passes the Whoosh test. Logged in the Ads DB with routing + hypothesis.
