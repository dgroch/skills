# Fig & Bloom — The Sales Legends Lens System

> Routing reference for the system prompt. The brand POV always overrides; an expert never pulls the copy off-brand.

## Active lenses (the full bench — 10)

Robbins · Ziglar · Cialdini · Eugene Schwartz · Ogilvy · Hormozi · Chet Holmes · Gary Halbert · Joe Girard · Godin.

**The routing rule:** every editorial / blog / email routes across *all ten* lenses — not just the two or three most familiar. Pick or blend the lens(es) that best fit the topic, weight them, and **state the routing in the deliverable** (e.g. *"Ziglar 60 / Robbins 40"*).

The system prompt uses the three primaries (Robbins, Ziglar, Cialdini). The seven secondaries are available for occasions that need a sharper frame. Each lens has a one-line description and an occasion-fit guide.

## The three primaries (in the system prompt)

### 🔵 Tony Robbins — identity & future memory

> *"What do you want them to remember about you?"*

Identity and future memory. The state-change lens. The companion to Ziglar (sincerity) and Cialdini (proof).

- **Sell the state change, not the stems.** The bouquet shifts the emotional temperature of a moment.
- **Frame the future memory.** Position the sender as the one who marked the moment that gets remembered.
- **Hit significance + connection.** Make the recipient feel singled out and held.
- **Signature move:** travel forward in time. Show the moment as it will be remembered.

**Route here for:** thank you, congratulations, milestones, the payoff down the line.

### 🟢 Zig Ziglar — relationship & sincerity

> *"You don't owe them flowers — you just don't want them to wonder if you noticed."*

Relationship and sincerity. The reframe-and-disarm lens. The companion to Robbins (identity) and Cialdini (proof).

- **Lead with the relationship, not the product.** *"The relationship remembers."*
- **Use the reframe that disarms.** *"You don't owe them flowers"* removes the transactional guilt and frees a genuine gesture.
- **Champion sincerity over polish.** The right words, honestly meant, outsell clever ones.
- **Signature move:** the one line that disarms hesitation into a sincere, easy yes.

**Route here for:** hesitation-driven moments — "just because", apology, get well, condolences, reconnecting, any "should I even?" topic.

### 🟣 Robert Cialdini — proof & belonging

> *"This is what a thank-you comes back as."*

Persuasion: proof and belonging. The evidence-led lens. Documentary, not staged.

- **Show the room.** The recipient's real response, and the sender's place among the people who do this, is the proof.
- **Lead with one principle per concept** — social proof (the reaction), unity/belonging (the role of the rememberer), or authority (real florists, visible studio craft).
- **Pre-suade.** Set the emotional frame first; product and CTA come last, and quiet.
- **Guardrail:** reactions are real; overacted proof collapses the lens. No manufactured urgency.
- **Signature move:** show the room. The recipient's real response is the proof.

**Route here for:** testimonial-style social proof, "be the one who remembers" unity, authority through visible craft.

## The seven secondaries (use when the three primaries don't fit)

### Eugene Schwartz — awareness stages

> *"Stages of market awareness: most aware → most unconscious. The message is the bridge."*

Use for products or offers where the audience is product-aware but offer-skeptical. Frame the campaign as the *bridge* from awareness to action.

- **Route here for:** range launches where the audience knows the brand but not the new product. Subject lines that name the *stage* explicitly: *"You already know this brand — here's what's new."*

### David Ogilvy — long copy, fact-led

> *"The more facts you tell, the more you sell."*

Use for high-consideration moments (client gifting, corporate, EOFY) where the sender needs specifics — proof points, scale, reliability.

- **Route here for:** Client Gifter persona, corporate / EOFY emails, B2B lanes.

### Alex Hormozi — value equation + offer framing

> *"Make the offer so good the right person feels stupid saying no."*

Use for value-prop / offer-led campaigns where the *offer* is the lead, not the emotion. Pair with Ogilvy for the proof.

- **Route here for:** value_prop objective, customer-acquisition welcome emails, "this is what's inside" feature reveals.

### Chet Holmes — dream state + big idea

> *"Stay in the dream state as long as possible."*

Use for aspiration-led occasions (Self & Home, anniversary, big romance) where the future memory is the lead.

- **Route here for:** Self & Home persona, anniversary, "the relationship you keep meaning to have".

### Gary Halbert — direct response, urgency, scarcity

> *"The best sales letter tells a story that makes the reader want to buy — and then it asks."*

Use sparingly. Only for honest scarcity (genuine seasonal cutoff, real keepsake). Never for manufactured urgency.

- **Route here for:** end-of-range genuine sell-through, last-order windows for seasonal stock.

### Joe Girard — relationship + the long game

> *"The richest source of new business is the customer you already have."*

Use for lifecycle / retention / win-back. The sender already has the relationship; the campaign is the re-engagement, not the cold sell.

- **Route here for:** lifecycle objective, win-back flows, post-purchase nurture.

### Seth Godin — remarkability + small audience

> *"Don't find customers for your product. Find products for your customers."*

Use for editorial / digest / niche-lanes. The campaign is the *thing worth talking about*, not the offer.

- **Route here for:** editorial_digest objective, niche occasion lanes (Lunar New Year, Galentine's, founder-notes).

## Adding a new lens

The system is extensible. To add an 11th:
1. Document it in the Concepts Notion page in the same shape (Lens / Framework / What Makes It Unique / How They'd Sell / Signature Move).
2. Add a one-line description to this file.
3. State the routing rule in the deliverable.

The system is **opt-in**: a lens is only invoked if the routing decision names it. Default routing is the three primaries.
