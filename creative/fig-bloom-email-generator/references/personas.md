## Persona 1: The Congratulations Sender

**Occasion:**
Congratulations

**Key Motivations:**
• Do the moment justice
• Be seen celebrating them ('the whole office saw who sent those')
• Share the joy

**Messaging Angles:**
• Big news deserves more than a like
• The whole office saw who sent those
• Match the gift to the moment

**Primary Problem:**
Standard bouquets underwhelm for a big moment. They want something that does the news justice and gets noticed — often in front of others.

**Top Objections:**
• Is it impressive enough — and will it arrive as full as the photo? (under-delivering in front of the office is the real fear)
• Will it be on time for the celebration?
• Will I get what I picked, or a substitute?

**Tagline:**
Big news deserves more than a like.

**Tier:**
Tier 1

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — honest scale, no shrinkflation surprise
• Statement designs, real craft
• Seasonal, florist-selected — swaps hold style and value, flagged up front
• On-time-or-free; same-day; fast human fix if it's off
• Reaction and social proof

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Umbria (statement) + size-up + premium card
• Occasion stacks: new baby, new job, engagement

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Celebrate someone's milestone visibly and generously — new job, baby, engagement, win.

## Persona 2: Self & Home

**Occasion:**
Self & Home

**Key Motivations:**
• Beauty and calm at home
• Identity — the person who keeps a beautiful home
• A small ritual that's hers

**Messaging Angles:**
• When did you last buy flowers for no one but yourself?
• A house feels different with Fig & Bloom in it
• Habit, not splurge

**Primary Problem:**
She treats flowers as a splurge for others, not a habit for herself. 'Is it worth it for just me?' — and 'will they last?'

**Top Objections:**
• Is it worth it for myself? (guilt)
• Will they last? (longevity is a top category driver)
• Will they look like the photos, or smaller?
• I don't want to commit to a subscription

**Tagline:**
Doesn't wait to be given flowers.

**Tier:**
Experimental

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — full and fresh as shown
• First-vase-free trial; skip anytime
• Freshness and longevity (reviews reward "lasted nearly two weeks")
• Everyday-luxe pricing; fast human fix if a delivery disappoints

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Flower Club subscription anchored on Lucerne
• Low-commitment trial; skip / pause flexibility

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Make her own space feel considered — a small, recurring luxury.

## Persona 3: The Bestie Send

**Occasion:**
Friendship

**Key Motivations:**
• Strengthen a close friendship
• Surprise her, make her feel seen
• Be the kind of friend who does this

**Messaging Angles:**
• Flowers for the friend who'd do the same
• For your person who isn't your partner
• Send it for no reason — that's the reason

**Primary Problem:**
Friendship gifting is less scripted than romance or family. She's unsure if flowers are too much, but wants to show up bigger than a text.

**Top Objections:**
• Is this too much for a friend?
• Will it actually look nice — and like the photo?
• Will I get what I picked, or a seasonal swap?

**Tagline:**
Flowers for the friend who'd do the same.

**Tier:**
Tier 2

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — full and fresh, not a letdown
• Playful, design-led arrangements that feel personal, not formal
• Seasonal, florist-selected — swaps hold style and value, flagged up front
• Same-day; on-time-or-free; fast human fix if it's off
• Recipient-reaction proof

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Everyday-luxe to mid design (Lucerne) + a fun card
• 'Just because' framing
• Two-pack (one for her, one for you) — worth testing

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Tell a close friend she matters — love, support, 'you've got this,' or no reason at all.

## Persona 4: The Sympathy Sender

**Occasion:**
Sympathy

**Key Motivations:**
• Be present for someone hurting, even from afar
• Convey care with dignity, without overstepping
• Relief that it landed, on time, looking right

**Messaging Angles:**
• When there's nothing right to say
• Let the flowers carry what words can't
• Quiet and dignified — designed for the moment, not stamped with an occasion

**Primary Problem:**
Doesn't know what to say or send, and fears getting the tone wrong — or it arriving late or tired at an already painful moment.

**Top Objections:**
• What if it's late — at a funeral, timing is everything and a miss is mortifying
• What if it looks cheap or wrong for the moment, in front of grieving family
• Will it actually look like the photo?
• Will I get what I chose, or a seasonal substitute?

**Tagline:**
When there's nothing right to say.

**Tier:**
Tier 1

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — same scale and fullness as shown
• On-time-or-free — timing guaranteed when it matters most
• Seasonal, florist-selected — if a stem swaps, the look and value hold, and you're told up front
• If anything's off, a fast, human fix — we own it, no defensiveness
• Designed by real florists; real outcomes ("delivered to our grieving friend… beautiful")

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Restrained, dignified design (never bright/celebratory)
• Card-message help with suggested wording — people freeze on what to write
• Clear funeral-home or home delivery options

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Show up for someone grieving when words aren't enough.

## Persona 5: The Thank-You Sender

**Occasion:**
Thank You

**Key Motivations:**
• Acknowledge someone properly
• Strengthen the relationship
• Be remembered as gracious

**Messaging Angles:**
• Thank you, said properly
• Some thank-yous deserve more than a text
• For the person who went out of their way

**Primary Problem:**
'Thanks' feels insufficient for what someone did. They want the gesture to match the gratitude without being over-the-top.

**Top Objections:**
• Is flowers the right call — and enough?
• Will it look considered, not generic — and as full as the photo?
• Will I get the arrangement I chose, or a seasonal swap?

**Tagline:**
Thank you, said properly.

**Tier:**
Tier 1

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — looks considered, reflects well on the sender
• Designed-not-assembled by real florists
• Seasonal, florist-selected — swaps hold style and value, flagged up front
• On-time-or-free; same-day; fast human fix if it's off

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Mid-to-statement design, scaled to the weight of the thanks
• Handwritten card prompt
• Thank-you occasion bundle

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Express real gratitude in a way a message can't.

## Persona 6: The Romance Sender

**Occasion:**
Romance

**Key Motivations:**
• The reaction when they open the door
• Being seen as thoughtful, not obligatory
• Marking 'us' — anniversary, just-because, making up

**Messaging Angles:**
• I know no one's done this before (first-timer)
• When you care how it lands
• You don't owe them flowers — you just don't want them to wonder if you noticed

**Primary Problem:**
Wants a real reaction and fears a generic, phoned-in bouquet — especially first-timers with no reference point.

**Top Objections:**
• I don't even know what to pick
• What if it looks nothing like the photo — and I look like I phoned it in
• I'm ordering it for those specific flowers — will I actually get them?
• What if it's late and I'm not there to check? (long-distance overlay)

**Tagline:**
For the one they're still trying to impress.

**Tier:**
Tier 1

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — same scale and fullness, no letdown
• A best-seller, can't-go-wrong hero design
• Seasonal, florist-selected — swaps hold the style and value, flagged up front
• Same-day + on-time-or-free for the surprise; fast human fix if it's off
• Real reaction footage

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Osaka (A$139) as the confident default + size-up nudge
• Add-on stack: handwritten card, vase, chocolates
• First-timer framing: 'the one to send if you've never done this'

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Show a partner they're loved and thought of — first time or hundredth.

## Persona 7: The Client Gifter

**Occasion:**
Corporate

**Key Motivations:**
• Reputation — be remembered
• Ease and repeatability
• Reliability for people they can't afford to disappoint

**Messaging Angles:**
• Or something they remember
• Clearly not a hamper
• For the people your business can't afford to disappoint

**Primary Problem:**
Default gifts (wine, hampers) are forgettable. They want tasteful and reliable at volume — something that reflects well on the company.

**Top Objections:**
• Is it reliable across multiple sends? A late or dead-on-arrival gift is a professional embarrassment
• How does invoicing, scale and repeat ordering work?
• Will it look corporate-naff — or as premium as the photo?
• Will recipients get consistent arrangements, or random substitutes?

**Tagline:**
Past the point of another wine hamper.

**Tier:**
Tier 2

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — premium and consistent, not hamper-grade
• On-time-or-free at volume; fast human fix, we own it
• Seasonal, florist-selected — swaps hold style and value, flagged up front
• Account/repeat-order ease; five-star service record

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Umbria as the standing client-gift design
• Scheduled / multi-send corporate cadence
• Branded card option

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Strengthen a client or staff relationship with a memorable, on-brand gift.

## Persona 8: The Birthday Sender

**Occasion:**
Birthday

**Key Motivations:**
• Be the one who actually made an effort
• Hit the day exactly
• Delight and surprise — 'they remembered'

**Messaging Angles:**
• Anyone can type 'HBD'
• Make the day, don't just mark it
• The gift that beats the group-chat message

**Primary Problem:**
A text feels lazy. They want to stand out — but it has to land on the day, not the day after.

**Top Objections:**
• Will it arrive on the actual day? A day late is worse than nothing (late delivery is the category's #1 complaint)
• Is it worth it over a cheap bunch — will it look the part?
• Will it match the photo, or arrive smaller?
• Will I get what I picked, or a substitute?

**Tagline:**
Anyone can type 'HBD.'

**Tier:**
Tier 1

**What Convinces Them:**
• Arrives true to the photo — scale and fullness as shown
• On-time-or-free — lands on the day, guaranteed
• Seasonal, florist-selected — swaps keep the look and value, flagged up front
• Same-day Mel/Syd/Bri; fast human fix if anything's off
• Reaction proof

**Offers & Merchandising:**
• Bright, celebratory hero + size-up
• Card + chocolate/balloon add-on stack
• Genuine same-hour panic routes to Whoosh, not here

**Core Job To Be Done:**
Make someone feel genuinely remembered on their day.
