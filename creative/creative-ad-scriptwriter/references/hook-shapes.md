# Hook shapes — recognition, not noise

The pattern interrupt stops the right person by **voicing what they already feel
or think**, spoken to one avatar, present tense, second person. Never the talent
narrating themselves. The hook can take many shapes; the template is not the
point — **resonance is**.

## The resonance test (the gate)

For every hook, ask: **would the right person feel "that's me"?** If it's a
claim, a flash, a discount, or something that could run on a Whoosh ad, cut it.
The recognition *is* the interrupt.

## The shapes (write across several)

1. **Name a feeling they're holding**
   - "You're still waiting for someone to send them."
   - "You've been meaning to, for three weeks now."

2. **Voice a private thought**
   - "A text feels too small for this."
   - "You don't want to get this one wrong."

3. **State a tension** *(you want X / you've got Y — one shape, not the rule)*
   - "You want to be in the room. You're three states away."
   - "You want it to feel like you. It usually feels like a last-minute order."

4. **Reflect a belief or identity**
   - "You're the one who always remembers."
   - "You're not a flowers person. You're a *means it* person."

## Right vs wrong

✅ Right — spoken to the avatar, voicing their state:
> "You're still waiting for someone to send them."

❌ Wrong — talent narrating themselves:
> "I've never been the flowers guy."

❌ Wrong — claim / hype / discount (would work on a Whoosh ad):
> "Australia's freshest flowers, 40% off this weekend only."

## Pairing rules

- The named bouquet is visible by ~second 2 — feeling and product land together.
- Steps 1 (pattern interrupt) and 2 (self-recognition) fire together; the callout
  *is* the recognition. Don't write them as separate beats.
- Tune the hook's emphasis to the lens routing (e.g. Girard → relationship/return;
  Robbins → the future memory; Ziglar → sincerity and reassurance) without
  breaking the second-person recognition.
- Match one avatar only. If a hook would fit two senders, it isn't drilled enough.

## Occasion sensitivity

For tender occasions (Apology, Get Well, Sympathy/Condolences), the hook names a
quiet, real feeling — presence, not performance. No urgency, no offer in the
hook; let presence be the proof and hold the guarantee for a soft close.
