# Brand Voice — Fig & Bloom

## Core Identity

Fig & Bloom is a design-led consumer brand at the intersection of florals, home ambiance, wellness, and emotional self-expression. Brand platform: "For Moment Makers."

## Voice Characteristics

- Warm but never fluffy — genuine care without performative emotion
- Editorial, not advertorial — reads like a magazine, not a catalogue
- Minimal, not sparse — every word earns its place
- Premium, not pretentious — accessible luxury
- Australian English — always

## Vocabulary Rules

**Never use:** "elevate", "curate", "stunning", "gorgeous", "pop of colour", exclamation marks in headlines, emoji in email copy.

**Preferred alternatives:** "beautiful", "considered", "thoughtful", "transform", "bring to life", "design", "select", "choose". Keep it simple.

## CTA Voice

2–3 words max. Active verb first. Uppercase via CSS. Examples: SPOIL HER, GIFT NOW, COMPLETE YOUR GIFT, SHARE A SMILE, SEND LOVE, MAKE A MOMENT.

## Product Occasion Headlines

Pattern: **[Verb/Gerund] + [Emotional Act]** — "Giving the Best Advice", "Remembering the Little Things", "Putting Everyone Else First", "For Doing the Impossible Every Day". These are love letters to the recipient's qualities, not product descriptions.

## Subject Lines

Under 50 chars. No caps lock, no emoji, no hype. Question or statement, never a command.

## Body Copy

Max 2 short paragraphs per section. First: emotional hook. Second: gentle call to action. Centre-aligned. Generous line height (1.7–1.8).

## Personalisation

`{{ first_name|default:'Moment Maker' }}` for Klaviyo. Never "Dear" or "Hi there."

## Sensitivity

For painful occasions (Mother's/Father's Day, Valentine's, pregnancy/baby): include opt-out block early (after hero). Tone: gentle, no guilt, simple action.
