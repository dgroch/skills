# Creative Social Grid Planner — Fig & Bloom

## Overview

This skill creates deliberate Fig & Bloom social grid chapters, especially **9-tile** and **12-tile** Instagram/feed plans. It translates a campaign theme into a cohesive visual world and a tile-by-tile production brief.

The core principle:

> Build campaign worlds, not isolated product posts.

For Fig & Bloom, the grid must sell **emotional certainty**: the sender feels confident, the recipient feels what was meant, and the product proves the promise without becoming the whole story.

## When to Use

Use this skill when asked to:

- create a 9-tile or 12-tile Instagram grid;
- create a social media grid/chapter/feed plan;
- plan Fig & Bloom organic social around an occasion, bouquet, campaign, or blog/email theme;
- turn a comms calendar into social tiles;
- brief a Social Media Manager, photographer, designer, or AI image agent for grid production;
- make the feed more cohesive while avoiding a product-only florist account.

Do **not** use this for:

- single Reel cover extraction from a video URL — use `creative-instagram-reel-cover`;
- full Klaviyo email build — use `creative-email-campaign-builder`;
- static product/lifestyle image generation only — use `creative-brand-photographer`;
- copy QA only — use `creative-copywriting-qa`.

## Fig & Bloom Strategy

### Brand job

Fig & Bloom is not merely selling bouquets. It is selling the feeling that the gift landed properly.

The social grid should answer at least two of these questions:

- Who is sending this?
- Why now?
- What do they hope the recipient feels?
- What did Fig & Bloom do to make it land properly?
- What message came back?

If the grid cannot answer these, it is probably too product-led.

### Avoid the product-only trap

Beautiful floral feeds can become unfollowable when every post is a bouquet/product surface. A good Fig & Bloom grid includes emotional and operational texture:

- sender anxiety / relief;
- recipient reaction or message-back;
- florist craft and accountability;
- delivery certainty / arrival proof;
- real home, studio, office, or doorstep context after delivery.

## Grid Ratios

### 9-tile chapter default

Use this as the default mix unless the brief says otherwise:

- 2 bouquet/product hero tiles;
- 2 human/sender/recipient/lifestyle tiles;
- 2 process/proof tiles;
- 1 macro texture tile;
- 1 typography/editorial statement tile;
- 1 environmental/lifestyle tile.

### 12-tile chapter default

Use this when the campaign needs more breadth, e.g. Mother's Day, EOFY gifting, sympathy, or a full May/June content run:

- 3 bouquet/product hero tiles;
- 2 human/sender/recipient tiles;
- 2 process/proof/delivery tiles;
- 2 macro/sensorial texture tiles;
- 2 editorial/education/blog-link tiles;
- 1 environment/lifestyle/seasonal mood tile.

## Campaign World Checklist

Before planning tiles, define the campaign world:

- **Theme / occasion:** e.g. birthday, Mother's Day, EOFY thank-you, sympathy, winter warmth.
- **Emotional arc:** sender need → Fig & Bloom certainty → recipient feeling / message after.
- **Palette:** 3–5 colours from bouquet + environment.
- **Textures:** linen, stone, timber, card, ribbon, vase, wrap, doorframe, phone, delivery detail.
- **Human texture:** hand, sender phone, recipient shoulder, florist apron, handwritten card.
- **Proof texture:** product page, delivery moment, card detail, care/craft, message-back recreation.
- **Blog/email connection:** which tiles can point to a blog, guide, email campaign, or landing page.

## Output Format

Always produce:

1. **Campaign World** — mood, palette, motifs, locations/surfaces, emotional arc.
2. **Grid Rhythm** — 3x3 or 3x4 map with tile type labels.
3. **Tile-by-Tile Brief** — for each tile:
   - tile number;
   - role/type: product, human, proof, macro, typography, lifestyle, blog-link;
   - purpose;
   - art direction;
   - asset source guidance: owned asset / new shoot / AI prompt / graphic;
   - caption hook;
   - CTA or internal link target if relevant.
4. **Production Notes** — shot list, copy notes, asset needs, and approvals.
5. **Anti-Drift Checks** — how the chapter avoids product-only sameness.

## Tile Type Definitions

- **Product hero:** honest bouquet scale, polished but not ecomm-flat.
- **Human:** sender/recipient/florist presence, hands, card writing, home context, reaction.
- **Proof:** delivery detail, studio process, wrapping, order confidence, message-back.
- **Macro:** petal, stem, ribbon, card, wrap, water, vase, texture.
- **Typography:** one strong editorial line, restrained layout, no generic quote-tile clutter.
- **Lifestyle:** home, office, bedside, dining table, doorway, studio, seasonal atmosphere.
- **Blog-link:** social tile that tees up a blog/email theme without feeling like a generic promo.

## Fig & Bloom Visual Guardrails

Do:

- soft documentary/editorial feel;
- warm clean whites, neutral-earthy palette, matte grain;
- natural daylight, soft shadows, real homes/studios/doorways;
- honest bouquet scale and product-to-delivery continuity;
- hands, cards, phones, ribbons, wrapping, vessels, delivery details;
- grid rhythm: wide / macro / human / proof / type.

Avoid:

- over-saturated product montages;
- flat ecommerce cutouts as the main feed language;
- AI-looking perfect people or fake reactions;
- generic luxury still life with no sender/recipient context;
- text-heavy Canva quote tiles;
- glossy HDR florals, hard studio strobes, neon greens, beige/yellow casts;
- invented logos, fake UI, or hallucinated props.

## Useful Editorial Lines

Use sparingly, one idea per typography tile:

- `For the moment they feel what you meant.`
- `When words are hard.`
- `The message after matters.`
- `Sent with care. Delivered with certainty.`
- `Not just flowers. The feeling after.`
- `Know what you sent. Know it arrived beautifully.`

## Prompt Template

```text
Create a [9/12]-tile Instagram grid chapter for Fig & Bloom.

Campaign theme: [occasion/product/brand idea]
Goal: [awareness/product launch/seasonal campaign/proof of quality/blog support]
Audience: [sender type]
Mood: [3–5 words]
Assets: [owned assets only / AI prompts / mixed / shot list only]
Blog/email links: [which blog posts or email themes should be supported]

Output the campaign world, grid rhythm, tile-by-tile art direction, caption hooks, asset guidance, CTAs, and anti-product-only checks.
```

## Owned-Asset Rule

If the brief says to use existing/owned Fig & Bloom assets, do not generate replacement imagery. Query the asset source or request the asset manifest, select suitable images, crop/package them, and preserve provenance. AI may be used for selection, QA, or light finishing only when explicitly allowed.

## Calendar Integration

When a communications calendar includes blogs and emails, social tiles should not be isolated. Link the grid chapter to the calendar:

- blog posts become education/proof/link tiles;
- emails can link to the blog and reuse the same emotional hook;
- social captions should tease the blog rather than duplicate the email;
- the grid should maintain visual rhythm across weeks, not reset every post.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Grid has a clear campaign world.
- [ ] Product/human/proof/macro/type/lifestyle mix is balanced.
- [ ] At least two tiles carry sender/recipient emotional context.
- [ ] At least one tile carries craft/proof/delivery certainty.
- [ ] Blog/email links are explicit where relevant.
- [ ] No tile relies on hallucinated UI, fake messages, or fake recipient reactions.
- [ ] Output is production-brief ready for a Social Media Manager or creative agent.
