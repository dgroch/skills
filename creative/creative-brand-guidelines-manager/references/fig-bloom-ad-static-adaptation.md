# Fig & Bloom Static Ad Adaptation from External References

Use this when Daniel provides an external ad/design reference and asks for Fig & Bloom statics.

## Core rule

Treat the reference as a **structural design source**, not merely an idea prompt.

The correct adaptation preserves:

- composition and layout mechanics
- visual density and rhythm
- typography hierarchy
- framing/crop behaviour
- collage, phone/mockup, annotation, or card mechanics
- how the eye moves through the piece
- the original format’s “why it works”

Then replace:

- brand world with Fig & Bloom
- products with real Fig & Bloom bouquets/assets where possible
- copy with warm, intentional, non-salesy Fig & Bloom language
- palette/logo/type with Fig & Bloom rules

## Daniel correction to avoid

Do **not** flatten external references into generic, minimal “brand-safe” cards. That loses the value of the reference. The reference is useful because of its structure and visual behaviour, not just its high-level theme.

Bad adaptation:

- “Carousel colours” becomes a plain grid of product cards.
- “Moodboard” becomes a tidy brand collage with no reference rhythm.
- “Social proof” becomes a generic testimonial card.

Good adaptation:

- Keep the colour-loaded discovery mechanic, swipe/card structure, dark/light contrast, product clustering, and copy hierarchy — but rebuild with bouquets and Fig & Bloom language.
- Keep the moodboard/Pinterest-board feel — but use real bouquets, cards, vases, interiors, and moment copy.
- Keep the social-proof/commerce interface mechanic — but use reviews, delivery proof, product details, and customer moment context.

## Workflow

1. **Recover the reference first.** If the user asks for examples, deliver the recovered screenshots/JPEGs/contact sheet before making derivatives.
2. **Name each reference mechanic.** Example: colour finder, moodboard, social proof, complete moment, choice helper.
3. **Extract structure separately from content.** Write down what stays fixed: layout, hierarchy, density, crops, frame shapes, device mockups, annotation style.
4. **Map to Fig & Bloom moments.** Persona + pain + guide angle should drive copy.
5. **Use real product imagery.** Prefer live website/product assets or manifest assets over AI-invented bouquets.
6. **Apply brand constraints.** Florals hero; airy/simple where appropriate; Noir/Silk White/Clay base; product/season colours as accents; black/white logo only.
7. **QA against the reference.** Ask: would Daniel recognise this as structurally related to the supplied example? If not, revise before delivery.

## Fig & Bloom reference pattern bank

### Carousel Colours

Use for: colour/mood-led bouquet discovery.

Preserve:

- multiple strong product/colour panels
- sequential discovery feel
- customer need/persona mapping
- visual abundance

Fig & Bloom angle:

- “Find the bouquet by colour mood.”
- Soft romance / native warmth / white-green composure / vivid joy.

### Carousel Mood

Use for: aspirational lifestyle and emotional gifting.

Preserve:

- moodboard structure
- mix of product, scene, texture, and human/context shots
- curated “buy into the world” feeling

Fig & Bloom angle:

- “Send the feeling.”
- Birthday, grief, thank you, hosting, self-care.

### Social Proof / UGC Proof

Use for: trust and conversion.

Preserve:

- proof embedded in commerce/product context
- real-person or real-moment cues
- interface/review/price/product information as trust builders

Fig & Bloom angle:

- delivery photo + review + product recommendation
- “when you can’t be there” proof

### Complete Moment

Use for: bundles and add-ons.

Preserve:

- routine/kit/completion logic
- product constellation rather than single product
- clear role for each add-on

Fig & Bloom angle:

- bouquet + vase + card + candle
- “the whole gesture, considered”

### Choice Helper

Use for: reducing decision anxiety.

Preserve:

- decision tree / chooser mechanic
- strong segmentation
- simple product pathway

Fig & Bloom angle:

- “Not sure what to send?”
- choose by recipient, relationship, mood, or moment.

## QA checklist

Before delivering, check:

- Reference structure is recognisable.
- Florals remain the hero.
- Product images are real or clearly sourced.
- Copy reduces a customer anxiety, not just decorates the layout.
- Logo is black/white only and has clearance.
- Typography is close to brand hierarchy: elevated display + clean sans.
- No salesy/hype wording.
- No “cheap/fast/lasts” rational commodity framing unless deliberately in a product detail context.
- If source extraction was imperfect, disclose what was recovered and what is cropped/screenshot-based.
