# Critique Rubric

Score **every slide** 1–5 on the axes that apply, then give the deck an average. Be
specific and honest — name the gap on each slide and the fix. Always review at full
2160×2700 and zoom into type, edges, and where text meets image.

## The seven axes

| Axis | Asks | 5 looks like | 2 looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Does it stop the scroll / earn the swipe? | Cover opens a loop with one image + ≤8-word line | A summary that resolves itself; busy; generic |
| **Hierarchy** | One clear focal point, read in order? | Eye lands once, then support | Competing sizes; timid headline in a void |
| **Restraint** | Premium and considered, or trying too hard? | A few earned moments | Emphasis on every slide; loud |
| **Rhythm** | Does it vary from its neighbours? | Texture/placement shifts each slide | Same recipe repeated; back-to-back type slides |
| **Image craft** | Crop, grade, type-on-image contrast | Horizontal hero, deliberate crop, legible type | Soft/awkward crop; type fighting a busy base |
| **Finish** | The details a reader feels but can't name | Clean breaks, curly punctuation, anchored base | Widows, straight quotes, unbounded void, faint folios |
| **Honesty** | Does the proof match the claim? | The product shot demonstrates exactly what the words assert | Image quietly contradicts the headline; product shown bigger/different than reality |

> **Why Honesty is its own axis.** A slide can score 4+ on all six craft axes and still
> fail: a beautiful product shot under a headline it doesn't actually demonstrate. The
> six craft axes don't catch it — only this one does. Read every photo slide's image
> *against its headline*, not just on its own merits. This is the brand's honesty
> principle (never depict product dishonestly) enforced at the slide level.

## The recurring "template tells" (hunt for these first)

1. **Invisible / faked emphasis.** A *turn* word that lands flat because emphasis was faked —
   a roman display face (Lust) set to a weight it doesn't have, or a synthesised slant. The
   "power word" then either looks clumsy or doesn't render as intended. Check at full res.
   Fix: real emphasis — **Cervanttis italic** (the brand's actual italic display face) or a
   palette colour shift — used rarely. Never substitute Neuzeit Bold for italic.
2. **One layout repeated.** Kicker + rule + big display + body, slide after slide,
   differentiated only by background colour. The single biggest "template not publisher"
   signal. Fix: vary texture and vertical placement; one accent per slide.
3. **No type ladder.** Many display sizes with no system; photo headlines half the size
   of text headlines so images whisper. Fix: ~3 deliberate steps; lift photo headlines in.
4. **Widows & width-breaks.** Lines break to the container, stranding single words. Fix:
   break by sense with explicit `<br>`; verify in the render.
5. **Straight apostrophes/quotes.** `'` and `"` instead of `’ “ ”`. Shows at display size.
6. **Slides doing the caption's job.** 3–4 line paragraphs on slides. Fix: headline on the
   slide, prose in the caption.
7. **Unanchored negative space.** Centred block floating with a large empty base and
   nothing holding it — reads unfinished. Fix: a quiet running foot or footer anchor.
8. **Folio/label contrast.** Page numbers invisible on photo slides. Fix: scrim or
   contrast tuned per background.
9. **Proof that contradicts the claim.** A product shot whose content undercuts the
   headline above it — e.g. "one foliage" over an arrangement visibly using three. The
   six craft axes miss this because the slide *looks* good; only the Honesty axis catches
   it. Fix: reshoot/recrop to a frame that demonstrates the claim, or change the claim.
   Never let the image quietly argue against the words.

## How to report

For each slide: the scores, then one or two sentences naming the **specific** gap and the
fix (not "could be stronger"). Then a deck average and a **ranked list of the highest-value
gaps** to close first — emphasis and rhythm usually top it. Finally, flag anything pulling
against brand POV — including any **proof-vs-claim mismatch** (a photo that contradicts its
headline) and over-emphasis fighting a "restraint" thesis — and give the on-brand alternative.

## Scoring guide

- **4.0+** publisher-grade; ship after polish.
- **3.0–3.9** competent but reads as brand template; close the top 3 gaps.
- **< 3.0** structural problems (repetition, hierarchy, finish); rebuild the weak slides.
