# Creative Communications Calendar Planner — Fig & Bloom

## Overview

This skill plans integrated Fig & Bloom communications calendars spanning:

- email / Klaviyo campaigns;
- organic social / Instagram grid chapters;
- blog/editorial content;
- supporting captions, CTAs, and production briefs.

The default planning pattern is **blog-led nurture**: many emails should drive to helpful or emotionally resonant blog content, while social turns the same themes into a grid/feed chapter with visual proof and sender/recipient context.

## When to Use

Use this skill when asked to:

- create a communications calendar;
- plan the remainder of a month and the following month;
- coordinate email, social, and blog themes;
- turn a strategic marketing idea into Paperclip issues;
- create a marketing manager brief with child issues for Email, Blog, and Social Media Managers;
- plan Fig & Bloom content around gifting occasions, seasonal moments, local delivery, or brand education.

## Planning Principles

### 1. One theme, three channel jobs

For each campaign/theme, assign a distinct job to each channel:

- **Blog:** teaches, frames, earns search/evergreen value, gives emails somewhere richer to link.
- **Email:** converts or nurtures; sends customers to blog, collection, product, or reminder action.
- **Social:** makes the idea visible and followable; uses grid rhythm, human/proof/product balance.

Do not make all three channels say the exact same thing.

### 2. Fig & Bloom brand job

Every calendar entry should support premium gifting certainty:

- sender confidence;
- recipient feeling;
- delivery/craft proof;
- thoughtful occasion framing;
- flowers as emotional outcome, not product commodity.

### 3. Blog-to-email linking

Many emails can and should link to blogs. Use emails as the prompt and blogs as the deeper answer.

Examples:

- Email: “Not sure what to write?” → Blog: card-message guide.
- Email: “The best flowers for winter birthdays” → Blog: winter birthday gifting guide.
- Email: “Client thank-yous that don’t feel generic” → Blog: corporate gifting guide.
- Email: “When words are hard” → Blog: sympathy/condolence flower etiquette.

### 4. Social grid integration

For social planning, call or follow `creative-social-grid-planner`. Each major theme should have either:

- a 9-tile grid chapter for focused themes; or
- a 12-tile chapter for month/season campaigns.

Social must include product, human, proof, macro, typography, and lifestyle balance. Avoid product-only florist posting.

## Calendar Output Format

Always produce:

1. **Strategic Frame**
   - date range;
   - primary audience segments;
   - key business goals;
   - brand idea / campaign promise.

2. **Monthly / Weekly Theme Map**
   - week/date;
   - theme;
   - emotional job;
   - product/collection tie-in;
   - blog/email/social roles.

3. **Channel Calendar**
   For each item:
   - date or week;
   - channel: email, social, blog;
   - title/hook;
   - audience;
   - content objective;
   - CTA/link target;
   - asset needs;
   - owner/agent role;
   - dependencies.

4. **Blog-to-Email Link Map**
   - blog title;
   - emails that should link to it;
   - social posts that should tease it;
   - product/collection CTA.

5. **Paperclip Issue Breakdown**
   - parent Marketing Manager issue;
   - optional child issues for Blog, Email, Social Media;
   - acceptance criteria and dependencies.

## Suggested Fig & Bloom Content Lanes

Use these lanes as inputs, not constraints:

- **Message after delivery:** the recipient response as the emotional proof.
- **When words are hard:** sympathy, apology, care, difficult moments.
- **Thoughtful birthdays:** personal, not generic.
- **Corporate/client thank-yous:** elevated appreciation without feeling templated.
- **Winter warmth:** flowers as home feeling, not just decoration.
- **Same-day certainty:** urgency without cheap sale language.
- **Behind the bouquet:** florist craft, stem selection, wrap, card, delivery.
- **What to write:** card-message education and copy prompts.
- **How to choose:** bouquet/occasion guides.
- **Local delivery trust:** Melbourne/Sydney/Brisbane context where appropriate.

## Paperclip Issue Template

Use this structure for the parent issue:

```markdown
# Integrated communications calendar: [date range]

## Intent
Create a Fig & Bloom communications calendar for [date range] spanning email, social media, and blogs. Many emails should link to supporting blog posts. Social should use the Fig & Bloom social grid chapter approach from `creative-social-grid-planner`.

## Company
Fig & Bloom

## Goals
- Plan the remaining [month] and [next month] content rhythm.
- Connect blog/editorial topics to email CTAs.
- Turn key themes into social grid chapters rather than isolated product posts.
- Maintain Fig & Bloom's premium gifting certainty positioning.

## Required Outputs
- Calendar table/list by date/week and channel.
- Blog-to-email link map.
- Social grid chapter briefs for major themes.
- Asset requirements and dependencies.
- Child issue recommendations for Blog, Email, and Social.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Emails have clear CTA/link targets, with blog links where appropriate.
- Blog topics support customer questions and evergreen/search value.
- Social briefs use product/human/proof/macro/type/lifestyle balance.
- Calendar is production-ready for channel owners.
```

## Child Issue Recommendations

Break out when execution will benefit from parallel agents:

- **Blog child issue:** draft June/remainder-May blog editorial plan and briefs.
- **Email child issue:** draft email campaign schedule, subjects, preview text, CTAs, and blog links.
- **Social child issue:** create 9/12-tile social grid chapters using `creative-social-grid-planner`.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Date range and company are explicit.
- [ ] Every email has a clear CTA/link target.
- [ ] Blogs are not orphaned; they support email/social themes.
- [ ] Social is planned as grid chapters where appropriate.
- [ ] Channel roles are distinct rather than duplicated.
- [ ] Output is ready to become Paperclip parent/child issues.
