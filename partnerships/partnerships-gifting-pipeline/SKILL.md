# Gifting Partnership Pipeline

This skill gives Broker a repeatable pipeline for finding and managing partners that improve Fig & Bloom programs without damaging margin, service quality, or brand trust.

## Trigger

Use when asked to:

- build a partner longlist or shortlist;
- scout AU brands for birthday, corporate, sympathy, or Bower bundles;
- draft partner outreach;
- track a partner deal;
- model partner unit economics;
- prepare handover from PR, Marketing, Ledger, Solace, or Bower Ops.

Run `reference-brand-separation-guardrail` for Bower work and `reference-spend-discipline-guardrail` before approving paid tests or commercial commitments.

## Partner Fit Criteria

Score each partner 1-5:

| Criterion | What Good Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Audience fit | Sends to the same occasion or buyer without confusing the brand |
| Product fit | Complements flowers, does not compete with core bouquet value |
| Operational fit | Ships reliably, can meet same-day or planned fulfilment constraints |
| Brand fit | Tactile, tasteful, Australian, not loud or discount-led |
| Margin fit | Leaves enough contribution margin after COGS, fees, refunds, and support |
| Legal/ethics fit | Clear permissions, no misleading endorsement, no risky claims |

Shortlist only partners with a total score of 22+ unless there is a documented strategic reason.

## Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|
| Longlisted | Basic fit and public contact found |
| Qualified | Scorecard complete, risks noted |
| Contacted | Outreach sent, date logged |
| Interested | Partner response confirms appetite |
| Commercial Review | Margin, fulfilment, support, and legal assumptions documented |
| Pilot Ready | Scope, SKU, dates, owner, and measurement plan agreed |
| Live Pilot | Tracking active |
| Scale / Hold / Stop | Decision made from pilot results |

## Unit Economics Model

For each proposed bundle, calculate:

```text
Bundle price
- bouquet COGS
- partner item cost
- packaging
- fulfilment/labour
- delivery
- payment/platform fees
- expected discount or credit
- expected refund/support allowance
= contribution margin
```

Then compare:

- contribution margin dollars;
- margin percentage;
- incremental CAC or partner fee;
- payback period;
- attach rate needed to break even;
- operational risk.

## Outreach Template

```markdown
Subject: Partnership idea for [occasion/program]

Hi [name],

I work with Fig & Bloom, a design-led Australian florist focused on gifting moments that need to land properly.

We are exploring [birthday/corporate/sympathy/Bower] bundles with a small number of complementary Australian brands. [Partner] stood out because [specific reason].

The first step would be a light pilot: [simple concept], measured on [metric], with clear brand and fulfilment ownership on both sides.

Would you be open to a short conversation next week?

Best,
[sender]
```

## Escalation

Escalate to the CEO for exclusivity, material spend, unusual liability, charity endorsement risk, long-term commitments, or any partner deal that changes strategic positioning.
